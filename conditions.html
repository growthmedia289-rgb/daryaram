Conditions We Treat

Expert diagnosis and treatment for a wide range of pain and musculoskeletal conditions.

Back Pain Treatment

Our pain management team focuses on diagnosing the source of back pain and developing personalized treatment plans aimed at reducing pain, restoring mobility, and improving daily function. Whenever possible, non-surgical treatment options are considered before surgical intervention.

Consult now

Neck (Cervical) Pain Treatment

Cervical pain refers to discomfort in the neck region that may radiate to the shoulders, arms, or upper back. It can develop due to poor posture, prolonged computer use, cervical spondylosis, injuries, or nerve compression. Our specialists assess the cause and recommend individualized treatment.

Consult now

Sciatica Treatment

Sciatica occurs when the sciatic nerve becomes irritated or compressed, causing pain that travels from the lower back into the buttock and down one or both legs. Treatment focuses on relieving nerve irritation, reducing pain, improving mobility, and preventing recurrence.

Consult now

Slip Disc Treatment

A slipped disc, also called a herniated disc, occurs when the cushioning disc between spinal bones bulges or ruptures, placing pressure on nearby nerves. Many patients improve with conservative treatment and do not require surgery.

Consult now

Spondylitis Treatment

Spondylitis refers to inflammatory conditions affecting the spine that can cause chronic pain and stiffness. Early diagnosis and proper management help preserve spinal movement and improve quality of life.

Consult now

Arthritis Treatment

Arthritis affects the joints and can lead to pain, swelling, stiffness, and reduced movement. Common forms include osteoarthritis and inflammatory arthritis. Treatment aims to reduce pain, improve joint function, and help patients remain active.

Consult now

Knee Pain Treatment

Knee pain can result from injury, arthritis, ligament problems, cartilage damage, or overuse. Proper diagnosis is essential for selecting the most appropriate treatment.

Consult now

Frozen Shoulder Treatment

Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis) is characterized by pain and progressive stiffness that limits shoulder movement. It commonly develops gradually and may take months to resolve without treatment.

Consult now

Neuropathic Pain Treatment

Neuropathic pain develops due to damage or dysfunction of the nervous system. Unlike muscle or joint pain, it often feels like burning, shooting, electric shock-like, or tingling pain.
