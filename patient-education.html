Health Articles & Patient Guides

Pain Management

Why Does Back Pain Keep Returning?

Back pain can keep returning when the underlying cause is not properly treated. Common reasons include poor posture, prolonged sitting, weak back muscles, slipped discs, arthritis, repetitive strain, or previous spinal injuries.

Guide

When Should You See a Pain Specialist?

You should consult a pain specialist if your back pain lasts for more than a few weeks, keeps returning, radiates to your legs or arms, causes numbness or weakness, or interferes with your daily activities.

Arthritis

Difference Between Arthritis and Rheumatism

Arthritis is a specific condition that causes inflammation, pain, and stiffness in one or more joints. Rheumatism is a broader term historically used to describe various conditions affecting joints, muscles, and connective tissues.

Treatment

Is PRP Treatment Effective?

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy may be beneficial for certain musculoskeletal conditions, such as some tendon injuries and mild to moderate osteoarthritis. However, its effectiveness varies depending on the condition being treated.

Spine Health

Understanding Sciatica

Sciatica is a condition caused by irritation or compression of the sciatic nerve, resulting in pain that radiates from the lower back through the buttock and down one or both legs. It may also be accompanied by numbness, tingling, or muscle weakness.

Prevention

How to Prevent Cervical Pain

Cervical pain can often be prevented by maintaining good posture, taking regular breaks from prolonged sitting, using an ergonomic workstation, avoiding excessive mobile phone use, and performing regular neck stretching and strengthening exercises.

Wellness

Managing Knee Pain Naturally

Mild knee pain can often be managed with regular low-impact exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, strengthening the muscles around the knee, and avoiding activities that place excessive stress on the joint.

Recovery

Frozen Shoulder: Symptoms & Recovery

Frozen shoulder is a condition that causes pain, stiffness, and a gradual loss of movement in the shoulder joint. Recovery typically occurs in stages and may take several months, depending on the severity of the condition.

Headache

Migraine vs Regular Headache

A regular headache usually causes mild to moderate pain and often resolves with rest or simple medication. A migraine is a neurological condition that typically causes intense, throbbing pain, often on one side of the head, and may be accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light or sound.

Prevention

Benefits of Preventive Health Checkups

Preventive health checkups help detect medical conditions at an early stage, often before symptoms appear. Regular screenings can identify risk factors for diseases such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, and certain cancers.

Nerve Health

Understanding Neuropathic Pain

Neuropathic pain is caused by damage or dysfunction of the nerves rather than an injury to muscles or joints. It is often described as burning, shooting, tingling, or electric shock-like pain and may be accompanied by numbness or increased sensitivity to touch.

Sports

Sports Injuries: Prevention & Recovery

Sports injuries can affect muscles, ligaments, tendons, and joints due to overuse, improper technique, or sudden trauma. Many injuries can be prevented through proper warm-up, strength training, appropriate equipment, and correct exercise techniques.
